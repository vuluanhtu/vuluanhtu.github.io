Projects

  • Modular Self-Reconfigurable Robotic System with Morphability

    Designed and developed a novel mobile, modular, and self-reconfigurable robotic system. 3D printed and assembled a functional prototype of 1 robot module, and conducted preliminary testing to validate the concept. This project was part of my Master’s thesis research at Purdue University Fort Wayne.

    • Researched and collected data from over 100 referenced research papers.
    • Designed a linear actuator and a gear transmission system following ASME standards to controll 6 degrees of freedom with only 2 DC motors.
    • Calculated power transmission capacity of gears, stack-up tolerances, and analyzed stress on critical components.
    • Simulated motion and stress FEA of the designed robot module in SolidWorks, and iteratively optimized the design based on simulation results.
    • Designed all parts for assembly including to be purchased components (motors, bearings, spring, bolts, nuts, and screws) and 3D printed them to assemble a physical prototype.
    • Presented findings in a 34000-word thesis.
  • Modular Attachment Adapter for Utility Terrain Vehicle

    Developed a modular attachment adapter for a utility terrain vehicle, enabling quick and easy attachment of various implements. A senior capstone design project sponsored by American Landmaster at Purdue University Fort Wayne.

    • Collaborated with other student engineers to design and build a modular lift mechanism for UTVs of a sponsor company with a $1000 budget.
    • Designed sheet metal parts and mechanical assemblies (45+ components) incorporating purchased off-the-shell components (linear actuator, springs, pulley, bearings) in SolidWorks.
    • Analyzed system kinematics, dynamics, stress, and summarized test data in a 115-page technical report.
    • Presented to a group of engineers at the sponsor company for system design improvements.
    • Created BOMs with complete cost of purchase materials and fabricated parts, and technical drawings with GD&T for metal fabrication.
  • Quadcopter Design with PID Control over Wi-fi

    Designed and built a quadcopter with PID control that can be operated on smartphones with Wi-fi connectivity. This is a personal project involved mechanical design, electronics integration, and control system development.

    • Designed the quadcopter frame and mechanical components in SolidWorks, ensuring structural integrity and weight optimization.
    • Integrated off-the-shelf components including brushless motors, propellers, Li-Po battery, ESCs, MPU6050 sensor, and Wi-fi module ESP8266 for remote control based on thrust, weight, and flight time requirements.
    • Developed a PID control algorithm from scratch using C++ in Arduino IDE to stabilize the quadcopter during flight, and uploaded it on Arduino Uno R3.
    • Optimized code runtime (<4ms) using ISR, Bitwise Operator, and Direct Port Access according to Arduino datasheet.
    • Conducted flight tests to tune the PID parameters and optimize performance, achieving stable flight and responsive control.
    • Designed a controller interface using RemoteXY app to control the drone on iPhone.
